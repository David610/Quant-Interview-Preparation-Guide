# Finance

## Market Making

Market making is the process of providing liquidity by continuously quoting:

- a **bid price** at which the market maker is willing to buy;
- an **ask price** at which the market maker is willing to sell.

The difference between these prices is the **bid–ask spread**:

\[
\text{Spread} = \text{Ask} - \text{Bid}
\]

Market makers aim to earn the spread while managing inventory risk, adverse selection, market risk, execution risk

A market maker must balance two competing objectives:

> Market makers do not predict every price move. They manage uncertainty by adjusting quotes as inventory, volatility and order flow change.
